Digging for Insight in Public Shipping Data

Thousands of shipments arrive into the United States everyday from every corner of the planet. Lumber. Gold slurry. flip flops, cell phones. Using Automated Manifest System (AMS) of the U.S. Customs and Border Protection (CBP) and published by Enigma, we can get peen into container vans for a granular look at the contents, customers, and commercial relationships that underpin our global economy.
Ports in Los Angeles, Long Beach, and New York dominate internationl container shipping in the United States. Ports along the Gulf Coast outpace the container ports by measure of tonnage thanks to the massive barges of grain floating down the Mississipi.

Analyzing the Enigma dataset for individual daily records shows tens of thousands of shipments being processed daily.

The economic scale of the ports is staggering. In 2016, $137 billion in Chinese and Hong Kong goods passed through the Port of Los Angeles alone.
